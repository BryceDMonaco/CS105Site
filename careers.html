There are many careers that I can take after I graduate as a computer science and engineering student.

Software Engineer I, Sierra Nevada Corporation

As a software engineer I at Sierra Nevada Corporation, I would be responsible for developing for systems, writing tests, and dealing with various forms of sensor data. To be a Software Engineer I at SNC, I would need at least a bachelors degree in computer science for my education, with some sort of professional experience preferred. Other requirements exist such as experience with designing and developing software, experience in defining projects, and the ability to work in a collaborative environment. At SNC I would likely be working on software handling sensor data, since that is what the Reno branch specializes in.

Software Engineer, 343 Industries (Microsoft)

As a software engineer at 343 Industries, I would be responsible for working with artists, designers, and other engineers to develop programs to assist in sandbox development. This essentially means making in house tools which make various processes of building games easier. To take this position, I would need at least a bachelors in computer science for my education, with at least two years of professional C++ or C# experience. 343 Industries also asks for at least two years working in a AAA game envrionment, which can be a difficult skill to add to your resume.

Software Engineer, Scientific Games

As a software engineer at Scientific Games, I would be working directly on making the latest casino games, especially variations of classic games such as blackjack or poker. To work as a software engineer for Scientific Games, I would need at least a bachelors in computer science or a related field and at least 1 to 3 years of experience with Unity or some other 3D game engine.
